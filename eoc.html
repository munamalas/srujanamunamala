    • EAST OAKLAND COLLECTIVE

    Case Study-UC Berkeley
    Tool used: Sketch
    Role: UX Researcher

    ABOUT

    The objective was to provide a responsive web design solution for a non-profit organization named East Oakland Collective. This organization strives to help individuals that are currently seeking housing and food as well as volunteers that are willing to dedicate their time.  East Oakland Collective offers two activities that volunteers can participate in called Weekly Hot Meal Prep and Feed the Hood.  The Weekly Hot Meal Prep program is a bi-monthly event which provides community members with large scale opportunities to donate money, items and time to provide food and necessities to unhoused populations across Oakland. Feed the Hood event volunteers are redirected to a Google form to register for the event.
    Weekly Hot Meal Prep has embarked on distributing 400 lbs of excess gourmet food from Silicon Valley and the South Bay to those in need in Oakland each week. Volunteers are asked to meet at a specific address and time, with the items they want to donate.

    RESEARCH

    We wanted our research to answer the following questions:

  • Why are people willing to volunteer at this organization?
  • Why do people choose to volunteer at these types of organizations?
  • What are the main pain points in determining where to find the appropriate forms?
  • Did anyone struggle with donating to the organization? If so, what was the problem?

    • We created a Google survey and sent it through 2 Facebook accounts. Out of the 21 people that completed the survey, 71.4% cared about homeless individuals. 60% of the people that donated to the organization were highly likely to volunteer there as well.

    We conducted a competitor analysis on local shelters similar to East Oakland Collective. We focused our research on how they optimized their donation flows. We researched three organizations: Lava Mae, Midnight Mission, and Street Sheet.

    USER PERSONA

    We analyzed the results from our survey and created two personas: Mary and John.

    Mary is a recent graduate of USF (University of San Francisco). She currently resides with two roommates and a cat named Pickle. She is passionate about social causes such as homelessness and wants to donate to a local non-profit organization.
    Her current challenges are the following:

  • Not knowing the impact of the donations.
  • Not having sufficient personal time.
  • Trouble keeping up with responsibilities

    • John is a program manager with a busy nightlife schedule. He would like to find a non-profit that he can volunteer for that can help create an impact on his community. His current challenges are the following:

  • Be part of the positive change in his community
  • Meet the like minded individuals passionate about social issues
  • Wants to feel like he is making a difference in people's lives
